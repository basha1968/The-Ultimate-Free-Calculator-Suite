How to Use This Calculator ▼

1. Enter Your Returns: In the text area, type your periodic returns. These can be daily, monthly, yearly, etc. They can be positive or negative numbers.

  • Percentages: Enter as `10%` or `10`. The calculator will automatically convert `10` to `10%` if no decimal point is present (e.g., `0.1` is treated as `0.1` decimal, while `10` is `10%`).
  • Decimals: Enter as `0.05` or `-0.02`.
  • Separators: Use commas (`,`) or spaces to separate your returns.

2. Calculate: Click the "Calculate Returns" button.

3. Review Results:

  • Arithmetic Mean: A simple average of your returns. Useful for understanding the typical return in a single period.
  • Geometric Mean: The true compound annual growth rate. This is generally the more accurate measure for investments, as it accounts for the effect of compounding.
  • Cumulative Return: The total percentage gain or loss over all periods.
  • Total Periods: The number of return periods you entered.

4. Export & Share: Use the buttons at the bottom to print, save as PDF/Excel/CSV, or share your results.
