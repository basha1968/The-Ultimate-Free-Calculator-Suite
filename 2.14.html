How the Euclidean Algorithm Works

The Euclidean algorithm is an efficient method for computing the greatest common divisor (GCD) of two integers. It's based on the principle that the GCD of two numbers also divides their difference.

Steps to calculate GCF using Euclidean algorithm:

  1. Start with two numbers, a and b, where a > b
  2. Divide a by b and get the remainder (r)
  3. Replace a with b, and b with r
  4. Repeat steps 2-3 until the remainder is zero
  5. The last non-zero remainder is the GCF

Example: For 48 and 60:
60 ÷ 48 = 1 remainder 12
48 ÷ 12 = 4 remainder 0
Therefore, GCF is 12
