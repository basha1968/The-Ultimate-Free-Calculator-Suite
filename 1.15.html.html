How to Use This Calculator ▼

This calculator helps you decide whether to prioritize cashback rewards or a low-interest loan when making a purchase.

  • Purchase Amount: Enter the full price of the item.
  • Cashback Rate: Input the percentage of cashback you'd receive if you paid with a cashback card (e.g., 2% for 2 cents back on every dollar). This option assumes you pay off the credit card balance before interest accrues.
  • Loan Interest Rate: Enter the annual interest rate for a low-interest loan you might use to finance the purchase.
  • Loan Term (Months): Specify how many months you'd take to pay off that loan.

Comparison Logic:

The calculator compares the total cashback you'd *receive* from one option against the total interest you'd *pay* with the low-interest loan option. It helps you determine if the cashback value outweighs the cost of interest, or vice-versa.

  • A **positive Net Financial Benefit** indicates that the cashback earned is greater than the interest you'd pay on the loan. The cashback option is financially more beneficial.
  • A **negative Net Financial Benefit** indicates that the interest paid on the loan is greater than the cashback you'd earn. The low-interest loan option is financially more beneficial (as it costs you less in interest than you would gain in cashback).
